Why Y Factor — the name is the method

Why is the Y first?

Every factory runs on four factors: people, machines, materials, methods. Most plants still run without the fifth — the question why.

The name says how we work. Take factory apart and pull the Y to the front; what remains is factor. Y Factor puts the question before the plant: why did the line slow, why did quality drift, why does Shift B run different. Then it answers, with the data attached.

FAQ — before a pilot

Questions plants ask before a pilot

Does our data leave the plant?

That's agreed per plant, in writing, before anything connects. A pilot can start with a scoped data path — cloud, VPC, or an on-prem conversation — and nothing moves until the path is on paper.

What if our machines are old?

We start on the sensors you already have. Where coverage is thin, we retrofit only that gap — OPC-UA, Modbus, and gateway options cover most older machines.

Who acts on an alert?

A person, every time. Agents watch, ask why, and recommend; your operators and supervisors decide. That's step 06 in the flow above, and it's there on purpose.

How long to a first signal?

We scope one line first and target measurable ROI inside 90 days. Useful signals usually land well before that — how fast depends on sensor coverage and process maturity.

What do you integrate with?

ERP and MES via API or export, PLCs and sensors over OPC-UA and Modbus, and SCADA or historian data. Named connectors are added per pilot.

How do you price a pilot?

Scoped per line and per problem. Book a demo and you leave with a written pilot outline — scope, timeline, and the price on it.
